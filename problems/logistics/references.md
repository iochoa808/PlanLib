## References

[Veloso92]: Learning by Analogical Reasoning in General Problem Solving
authors: Manuela M. Veloso
venue: PhD Thesis, School of Computer Science, Carnegie Mellon University, 1992
url: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~mmv/papers/92thesis.pdf
note: Introduces the Logistics domain in the context of the PRODIGY planning system

[McDermott98]: PDDL — The Planning Domain Definition Language (Version 1.2)
authors: Drew McDermott, Malik Ghallab, Adele Howe, Craig Knoblock, Ashwin Ram, Manuela Veloso, Daniel Weld, David Wilkins
venue: Technical Report CVC TR-98-003/DCS TR-1165, Yale Center for Computational Vision and Control, October 1998
url: https://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/mfourman/tools/propplan/pddl.pdf
note: Uses Logistics as the running example domain in both STRIPS and ADL formulations

[Bylander94]: The Computational Complexity of Propositional STRIPS Planning
authors: Tom Bylander
venue: Artificial Intelligence, 69(1–2):165–204, 1994
url: https://doi.org/10.1016/0004-3702(94)90081-7
note: Proves plan existence for general STRIPS planning is PSPACE-complete

[KoehlerEtAl97]: Extending Planning Graphs to an ADL Subset
authors: Jana Koehler, Bernhard Nebel, Jörg Hoffmann, Yannis Dimopoulos
venue: Proceedings of ECP-97, LNCS 1348, pp. 273–285, Springer, 1997
url: https://doi.org/10.1007/3-540-63912-8_92
note: Describes IPP, winner of IPC 1998 which included Logistics

[HoffmannNebel01]: The FF Planning System: Fast Plan Generation Through Heuristic Search
authors: Jörg Hoffmann, Bernhard Nebel
venue: Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research, 14:253–302, 2001
url: https://doi.org/10.1613/jair.855

[IPC98Archive]: IPC 1998 Logistics Benchmark Instances
